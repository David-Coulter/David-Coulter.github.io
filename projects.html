Water Predictive Analysis Program (Senior Capstone)

Continuous depletion of water from the Colorado River due to chronic overuse and continuous drought, necessitates more precise water management methods. Bard Water District (BWD) currently runs at 73% forecast accuracy. The design team developed a python-based program capable of forecasting the necessary amount of irrigation water for the entire district with the variables identified through a full factorial design of experiment. Based on factor weights derived from the analysis, the algorithm employs a weighted sum formula to forecast requirements. Inductive machine learning continuously refines factor weights based on updated actual consumption data.

Software Engineering Interactive Display

The purpose of this project is to provide an interactive way for students to receive information about the Software Engineering program at the University of Arizona. Additionally, this will help The University of Arizona Software Engineering department conduct an acceptance test to demonstrate that the Software Requirements Requiem Specification has been accepted. Our team worked with stakeholders in developing system requirements and then creating a test acceptance plan to validate and verify each requirement is met. Our program was written in the Java language and incorporated GUIs by using JavaSwing and JavaFX. The program allowed users to navigate through various GUIs to learn more about the software engineering programs offered at the University of Arizona, engage with other students and faculty through various clubs, and expand their knowledge in software engineering.
